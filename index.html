About Me

I am currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering at the University of Florida with a projected graduation date of May 2027. I maintain a 3.76 GPA and have developed expertise in both electrical engineering fundamentals and software development. I repaired my first phone when I was 12 and built my first computer when I was 13, leading me to a future in technology. Shortly after, I started teaching myself how to code and haven't looked back since.


Currently, I work as a researcher at the Smart Systems Lab at UF, where I'm the second author on research involving AI-powered human intention prediction with 40-60% accuracy. I also co-founded JustAsk software through Volera Technologies, an education app that uses AI to help professors understand student comprehension during lectures. Additionally, I have been working as a Field Technician for Building Automation Systems since 2021, gaining hands-on experience with HVAC systems and network troubleshooting.


Prior to my current roles, I worked as a Field Technician at General Electric Kitchen Appliances in Louisville, KY (August 2025 - December 2025), where I designed PCBs using Cadence Allegro software for plug-and-play solutions to program single-board computers on assembly lines. I also performed rigorous testing of hardware components under extreme conditions, including temperatures up to 80°C for extended periods, ensuring appliance reliability and optimal performance.



Beyond my academic and professional work, I serve as Vice President of the UF Table Tennis Club, where I've organized tournaments and managed fundraising activities. I'm also certified in ITF+, Lifeguarding with CPR/AED, and am a Citi Bank Scholar.
